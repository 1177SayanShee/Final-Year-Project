Early Blight Treatment Guide

Early blight is caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, which survives in soil and plant debris. It thrives in warm temperatures, high humidity, and alternating wet and dry conditions. Proper management is key to controlling this disease.

Treatment and Prevention Measures:

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves and plant debris immediately to reduce fungal spores.
  • Apply fungicides such as mancozeb, copper oxychloride, or those containing fluopyram as soon as symptoms appear. Always follow the product label instructions.
  • Use disease-free seeds or seed tubers to avoid introducing the fungus into your field.
  • Practice crop rotation yearly to break the disease cycle and reduce soil contamination.
  • Choose potato varieties with resistance or tolerance to early blight whenever possible.
  • Maintain plant health by providing proper fertilization, irrigation, and pest control to reduce stress susceptibility.
  • Harvest crops in dry conditions to minimize disease spread.
  • Sanitize gardening tools regularly, especially pruning shears, and avoid working on plants when wet.
  • Improve air circulation by pruning or staking plants to reduce humidity around foliage.
  • Use mulch to prevent soil spores from splashing onto leaves during watering or rain.
  • Water plants at the base rather than overhead to keep foliage dry and discourage fungal growth.
