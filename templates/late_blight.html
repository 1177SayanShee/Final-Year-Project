Late Blight Treatment Guide

Late blight is caused by the fungus Phytophthora infestans, which thrives in cool, moist environments and can quickly devastate potato crops. Effective management combines resistant varieties, sanitation, and fungicide use.

Treatment and Prevention Measures:

  • Destroy infected plants completely to prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Apply fungicides such as metalaxyl, fluopicolide, or protectants like mancozeb according to local extension recommendations.
  • Do not compost infected plant material; instead, dispose of it safely.
  • Remove volunteer potato plants that can harbor the pathogen.
  • Space plants properly and prune to improve airflow, reducing humidity around foliage.
  • Ensure proper soil drainage to avoid waterlogging which favors fungal growth.
  • Use balanced fertilization to keep plants healthy and more resistant to infection.
  • Harvest potatoes promptly and store tubers in a cool, dry place to prevent post-harvest infections.
  • Sanitize tools and avoid working on wet plants to reduce spread.
  • Consider copper-based fungicides or horticultural oils if organic options are preferred.
